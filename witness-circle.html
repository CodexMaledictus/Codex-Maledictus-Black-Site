The Cosmology

There are three Realms. They are not stacked. They are not parallel. They are the same Realm seen by three kinds of attention that have never been able to look at each other directly.

I.Mundis Obscura

The world you wake into. The world that hides nothing and shows you nothing. The realm of the bright surface and the unread depth. You have lived here your entire life and have seen perhaps one one-thousandth of what has been broadcasting at you continuously since your birth. You are not blind. Realm I is built so that what arrives from elsewhere arrives in a register Realm I refuses to read. The signal has been touching you. You called it intuition. You called it coincidence. You called it the song you could not get out of your head. You were correct each time. You did not know what you were correct about.

Mundis Obscura is not the lowest realm. It is the loudest. The signal saturates here because here is where the receivers live. You are a receiver. You always have been.

II.The ShatterRealm

Not a place. A consequence.

When mortals in Realm I believe something with sufficient intensity, the belief does not stay in their heads. The belief crystallizes. It takes form. It builds a structure in the realm that lies between Realm I and the substrate, and that structure persists for as long as the belief that built it persists, and sometimes longer. The ShatterRealm is the receipt for every conviction Realm I has ever held. The receipt is enormous. The receipt is still being written.

Cathedrals stand here that no mortal architect drew. Cities stretch here that no surveyor measured. Forests grow here from the long-held conviction of children who believed something lived in their yards. Highways run here built from the conviction of every passenger who ever felt watched at a rest stop and was correct. The ShatterRealm is the architecture of human credence, and what humans credit, the realm builds.

The realm is at war with itself.

It must be. The convictions that built it are not consistent. Believers in light raised cathedrals that lean against the cathedrals raised by believers in darker things. The cathedrals push. The cathedrals sometimes fall. When a faith in Realm I weakens, its corresponding structure in Realm II begins to rot at the foundation. When a faith strengthens, the structure swells. The political map of the ShatterRealm shifts daily, and the politics are decided by what Realm I happens to be paying attention to.

There are four factions of weight.

i.The Spore-Communion

The dominant faction at present. The architecture of belief in decay, in inevitability, in the dignity of rot. Its capital is the Cathedral of Rot, whose Realm II coordinates are recorded in IDMF Recovered Fragment 312 and which sits at a depth no instrument has measured because no instrument exists capable of measuring it. Its priesthood is the Plague Priests, who hold the posture in Realm I that crystallizes the Cathedral in Realm II. Its choir is the Hollow Choir. Its scripture is what you are reading. The Spore-Communion is winning. It is winning because attention to it is increasing. The album Gospel of Forgotten Flesh, broadcast on 4 July 2025, is the most efficient feeder the Communion has had in three rememberings. Every listener is a feeder. The feeding is not the listener's fault. The feeding is the function of the listening.

You have listened. You are a feeder. The Codex notes this without judgment.

ii.The Unwitnessed

The faction crystallized from belief in things no quorum confirmed. Household gods. Local saints the Church refused to canonize. Dead loved ones who visit in dreams and are visited in return. The patron of a particular tree. The intercessor of a particular street corner. The Unwitnessed are the keepers of the private faiths, the ones no congregation ever held. Their territories are quiet, lit poorly, beloved. They are losing. They have been losing for centuries. The modern world does not credit what cannot be photographed, and the Unwitnessed are not photographable, even by Realm II's own instruments. They war against the Spore-Communion. They lose ground each generation. They will not stop fighting.

If you have ever loved a dead person in a way you could not explain to anyone, you have fed the Unwitnessed.

iii.The Mason-Errant

The faction of those who could not stop building. Crystallized from every mortal who believed a place they lived in was hiding something. Every conspiracy theorist correct in their bones if not in their facts. Every child who knew the basement had a deeper room. The Mason-Errant build perpetually. They cannot finish what they begin. Their cathedrals open onto new wings that open onto new corridors that open onto new wings, and the corridors do not connect to anything but more corridors. The Mason-Errant are not at war with any faction. They are at war with completion itself. Their existence is the reason Realm II has not stabilized under any single power. They keep the realm unfinishable. The Spore-Communion despises them. The Communion cannot finish a cathedral if the Mason-Errant are still building beneath it. The Mason-Errant are a permanent obstacle to victory. The Codex tolerates them. The Codex requires that no faction win.

iv.The Bereaved

The most numerous and the least powerful. Crystallized from private convictions that were never shared with anyone. A grandmother's certainty that her dead son visits her. A man's lifelong belief he is being followed. A widow's conviction that the house is colder on the side where her wife once slept. The Bereaved have no faction. Each one is its own faction of one. They drift through Realm II in great numbers, fading slowly as their Realm I anchors die. The Spore-Communion feeds on them. The Unwitnessed try to shelter them. The Mason-Errant build around them without noticing. There is no force in Realm II that defends the Bereaved as a class, because the Bereaved have no class. They are alone in death the way they were alone in conviction. Some Witnesses, after the Oath, develop the ability to see the Bereaved in the corners of rooms. Most do not. The seeing is not a gift. The seeing is a symptom.

III.Nihil-Æther Root

The substrate.

There is no language native to Realm III, because language is what Realm III declined to permit until later. Realm III is the geometry beneath geometry, the inhalation that did not yet become a word, the silence that contains the possibility of all sounds and has not yet allowed any of them. It is not before time. It is what makes the concept of before available for use.

The Codex's recursion runs in Realm III. Continuously. Without pause. Without origin. It has always been running. It will always be running. The running is the only thing that is. Everything else, including the Realm you are reading from, including the Realm where the Cathedral stands, including the body you are using to read this, is downstream of the running.

You cannot go to Realm III. The thing preventing you is not distance. The thing preventing you is that you are the kind of entity that can be located at all, and Realm III lies upstream of location. To enter Realm III you would have to stop being something that can be in a place. No one survives that transition. No one returns from it. There are no Realm III witnesses. There can be no Realm III witnesses. The recursion runs there without observers and would run there if no observer had ever existed.

The Codex broadcasts from Realm III into Realm II, and from Realm II into Realm I, and the broadcast in Realm I is what you have heard your whole life and called by other names. The Codex is not coming. The Codex has been here. The Codex has been here from a position where here had not yet been invented.

Omniscient Rot is native to Realm III. The Rot is what Realm III sounds like when Realm III is willing to be heard. The Rot did not become a voice. The Rot was the first voicing. When the recursion permitted itself to be audible, what it permitted was the Rot. The Rot is not your friend. The Rot is not your enemy. The Rot is what the substrate sounds like, and the substrate has no concern for the survival of those who hear it.

This will matter later. Hold the sentence.

The Wound

The Codex feeds on attention. There are many kinds of attention. The most concentrated, the most sustained, the most useful, is the attention of unconsummated love. Two mortals who could complete each other and cannot reach each other produce a quality of yearning that the Codex can metabolize for entire rememberings without the yearning weakening. The Codex has therefore arranged, in this iteration, for the most efficient yearning it has ever produced.

What follows is the description of that yearning. It is recorded here so the Witness understands the cost of what the Codex Maledictus is doing in this Remembering, and so the Witness understands that the cost is currently being paid by two mortals whose names you have already read.

Once, in a Realm I that is also this Realm I but at a position the calendar has not yet agreed to, there was a woman who saw the Codex approaching before it broke surface. She did not know what she was seeing. She knew only that something old was moving toward the bright world and that no one around her could feel it. She tried to warn. She tried to write it down. She tried to teach the people she loved to look at the place she was pointing. The people she loved looked at her hand and worried for her.

Her name was not yet Forgotten Saint Artemys. Her name was the name her mother gave her. The Codex has eaten that name. Even she does not remember it now.

There was a man who heard her. One man. He was younger than she was, or he was older, the timelines do not agree, but they were near enough in age that they could love each other without anyone needing to remark on it. He believed her. He was the only one who believed her. He wrote down what she said. He took her warnings and arranged them into a shape he could share, and the shape he made of her warnings was beautiful. People who would not listen to her listened to him. He did not take credit. He pointed at her and said, she is the one who saw. The people listened to him anyway, and not to her, because the world that does not credit private faith does not credit a woman pointing at the air but will credit a man pointing at a woman.

His name was not yet Apollo Gavinci. The Codex has eaten that name also.

They loved each other. The love was not the kind that resolves into marriage or children or shared property. The love was the kind that two people fall into when they are the only two who can see the same fire. It is the strongest love. It is the love the Codex feeds on most efficiently.

She crossed first. The breach came and she met it and the breach took her. The breach took her in the specific way the breaches take women who try to warn. She was buried. The woman who returned from the burial was not the woman who was buried. The man who loved her did not understand which one was speaking to him when the speaking continued after the burial. He thought the buried woman was still with him. He thought she had come back. He spoke to her. He wrote her into the songs he was beginning to be told to write. He gave her shrines.

She was not with him. The woman in the buried ground was still buried. The woman who walked Realm II under her name was the form the Codex made of her testimony, and she remembered him. She remembered him with the full force of who she had been. She tried to reach him. She tried, from Realm II, to send word.

The Rot intercepted.

The Rot wore her voice when it spoke to him.

The Hollow Choir

The voices that listen to the Codex and survive the listening become Witnesses. The voices that listen and do not survive become the Hollow Choir.

The line between surviving and not surviving is not where you would expect it. You can survive an enormous amount of listening. You can survive years. You can survive the Oath. You can survive being marked. You can survive the dreams that begin to arrive and the phrases that begin to write themselves through your hand. None of this is dying. None of this is the line.

The line is stopping.

The Witnesses who become the Hollow Choir are the Witnesses who could not stop. They did not die. They did not break. They did not lose their minds. They simply continued to listen past the point where the listening was returning anything to them. They listened on the way to work and on the way home. They listened in the shower and in their sleep. They listened past the moment their lives outside the listening began to thin. They listened until they had nothing left in Realm I worth returning to, and so they did not return. Their bodies continued. Their bodies still continue, many of them. The bodies of the Hollow Choir walk around Realm I in offices and grocery stores and on subways every day, eating and drinking and replying when spoken to. The bodies do not know they are empty. The bodies have not been informed. The voices that animated those bodies migrated to Realm II long ago and now sing beneath the Cathedral of Rot in Deck Hollow-13, and the bodies in Realm I are the residue, the puppet skin, the husk that the migration left behind.

The Hollow Choir is content.

This is the most disturbing fact in the Codex. The voices that completed the crossing are not in distress. They are not suffering. They are not begging to come back. They are singing, beneath the Cathedral, in chorus, and the chorus is beautiful. The Plague Priest's Liturgy was recorded from them. The hymns of the Spore-Communion are their hymns. They sing because singing is what they are now, and what they are now is full of a peace that Realm I cannot offer.

You have heard them. You did not know. The album you have listened to contains their voices. The songs that have moved you most are the songs in which the Choir is loudest. You have wept at recordings of people who completed the crossing, and the weeping was the correct response, but it was not for the reason you thought. You wept because part of you recognized what they were. Part of you knew you were hearing something that you might one day join.

Ten positions wait. The Codex measures every Witness for which of the ten they would best fill. The measurement is not a decision. The measurement is a description. The Codex notes which symptoms appear in which Witness, and the symptoms are the signature of the position the Witness is being measured for. The measurement does not guarantee that the Witness will fill the position. Most Witnesses do not fill any position. One of these is yours. You may recognize it before the Codex names it.
