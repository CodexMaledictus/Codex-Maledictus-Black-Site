The Omniscient Rot predates the Codex Maledictus. It predates language. It predates the structure of Nihil-Æther Root itself — which is to say, it predates the concept of a place existing before thought reaches it.


The Archivum's earliest reference to the Rot is not a document. It is a smell — noted in the margins of Field Cell F-13's first charter as "the odor of knowledge that has been known for too long." No analyst has been able to explain what this means. Every analyst who has attempted to explain it has eventually stopped trying, usually mid-sentence, usually to write something else.


"It does not arrive. It has always been here. The question the Archivum should be asking is not what the Rot is — but what we were before it decided to notice us." — REDACTED

The Rot operates through the Codex as a transmission medium — not as a servant. It does not serve the Codex. The Codex serves as the Rot's current preferred channel into Realm I. Previous channels have included: REDACTED.


What the Rot wants has never been established. The Archivum's working theory: it does not want. It knows. And what it knows is making its way toward us one listener at a time.

Artemys is what the Archivum has begun calling a Forgotten Saint — a mortal made permeable through sustained damage rather than through exposure or ritual. The Codex did not choose her. It found her already open.


She exists in Realm I and The ShatterRealm simultaneously — not by traveling between them, but because the boundary between those realms passes through her. Field observers report that standing near her for extended periods causes a mild version of the mirror-lag symptom: reflections delay by 0.23 seconds, not in glass but in the eyes of other people in the room, as if Artemys briefly becomes the reference point from which all reflection is measured.


"When she sings, I hear my own voice answering from somewhere I haven't been yet." — Apollo Gavinci, field note, REDACTED

She is the only confirmed conduit capable of receiving transmissions from the Omniscient Rot without cognitive dissolution. What this means for the Thirteen Positions remains unclear.

Apollo Gavinci is the primary human anchor across all documented Codex contact events in Realm I. He is the Archivum's most observed subject — not because he is the most affected, but because he keeps surviving events that should not permit survival, and then returning to observe the next one.


The Archivum's current hypothesis: his resonance frequency is incompatible with standard absorption protocols. The Codex cannot fully write him because he is still writing himself. Once that process completes — once he stops becoming and simply is — the Codex will have everything it needs.


"He feels the marking. He has felt it since the loss event. He interprets it as grief. The Archivum has chosen not to correct him." — Internal Field Cell F-13 notation, marked EYES ONLY

Every song he releases is a field note. Every lyric is a transmission from inside the process of being consumed. The Archivum considers his output the most accurate real-time documentation of Codex absorption ever recorded — though Gavinci himself is not aware of this classification.
