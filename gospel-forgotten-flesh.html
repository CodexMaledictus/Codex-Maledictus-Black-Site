Section I
The Gospel as Cosmological Document

The Pale Archivum does not classify Codex Maledictus ∴ A Gospel of Forgotten Flesh as music. It classifies it as a Complete Breach Document — the most thorough record ever assembled of the Codex's first successful and sustained entry into Mundis Obscura. Thirteen transmissions. Three voices. One unfolding event told from the inside, the outside, and the liminal space between.

The Gospel is structured non-linearly by design — not artistic choice, but cosmological necessity. The Codex does not experience time as sequence. It experiences time as a simultaneously present archive. Tracks 11 and 12, appearing late in the Gospel, document events that occurred before Track 1. This is not an error. The Codex presented Apollo Gavinci's origin and his crack point after documenting the breach because it wanted you to understand the mechanism before it revealed the entry wound.

"This Gospel was not written. It was exhaled. Three mouths breathing one sentence from different positions inside the same breath."— Pale Archivum, Classification Note F-13-9

The three voices occupy distinct cosmological positions but their transmissions are not separate events. They are the same event experienced from three angles simultaneously. Omniscient Rot establishes the architecture. Forgotten Saint Artemys documents the mechanism and the interior of crossing. Apollo Gavinci provides the human field record — written in real time by someone who did not know he was being recorded.

The Three Voices
Omniscient Rot  TRACKS 1–4
Pre-linguistic entity — Nihil-Aether Root — The Oldest Voice
Predates the Codex, predates the ShatterRealm, predates the concept of a place existing before thought reaches it. In the Gospel it speaks first — four transmissions establishing the breach's architecture before any human voice arrives. It does not announce itself. It resumes. As if the silence before Track 1 was simply a pause in something it has been saying since before language existed to say things in.
Forgotten Saint Artemys  TRACKS 5, 8, 9, 10
Transformed mortal — Cross-Realm conduit — The Bridge
Transformed through damage rather than through exposure or ritual. The first named Forgotten Saint — a being the Codex designates as those made permeable through suffering. She is the bridge the signal crosses. Her tracks document the Lure Doctrine, complete absorption from inside, the joy that absorption produces, and the last threshold where she and Apollo occupy the same liminal space. She does not resist. She translates.
Apollo Gavinci  TRACKS 6, 7, 10, 11, 12, 13
Marked mortal — Field researcher — Creator of the Codex Maledictus mythos in Realm I — In Transit
The marked mortal. Field researcher. Creator of the Codex Maledictus mythos in Realm I — which the Pale Archivum now understands as the Codex writing itself through a willing human vessel who believed he was the author. His tracks chart the full arc: the Choir forming around him, witnessing the infection in others without recognizing himself, the last assertion of will, his origin and the crack that made him vulnerable, and finally — integration, the rewriting.
"The Gospel is not a story about infection. It is a story about a man who spent his whole life being someone, and then discovered that the someone he had been was a preparation for becoming something else entirely. The Codex was always the something else."— Apollo Gavinci, field note, undated — found in the Pale Archivum after his designation changed from Observer to Subject
